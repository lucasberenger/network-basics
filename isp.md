# Internet Service Provider

An ISP (Internet Service Provider) supplies the connection between a home network and the Internet. 

An ISP can be a local cable provider, a landline telephone service provider, a cellular operator that provides smartphone service, or an independent provider who leases bandwidth on the physical network infrastructure of another company.

ISPs are essential for communication on the Internet. Each ISP connects to other ISPs to form network links that interconnect users around the world.

Internet backbone is like a highway for data, providing high-bandwidth connections that links multiple service provider networks across major cities worldwide.

The main physical medium that connects the Internet backbone is the fiber optical cable. This cable is usually installed underground to connect cities within a continent. They also run under the sea to connect continents, countries, and cities.

### ISP Connections

ISP connections consist of a complex network of fiber optical cables, along with switches and routers that direct the information flow across source and destination hosts.

Home users are usually not aware of the infrastructure beyond their own network. They might think that connecting to an ISP is a fairly simple process.
